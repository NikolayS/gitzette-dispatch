Sqlever Reaches Critical Mass: 20 Pull Requests Merged in a Single Week

The Sqitch-compatible Postgres migration tool surges toward its first release as AI-assisted development delivers a torrent of features, tests, and fixes in seven days.
By The Dispatch · March 21, 2026

In what may be the most productive week in the young project's history, sqlever absorbed twenty pull requests from initial feature work through a comprehensive test suite and all the way to near-release polish. The pace — averaging nearly three merges per day — reflects a new mode of development in which human review and AI execution operate in tight collaboration.

The week's work covered the full breadth of the tool's ambitions. Zero-downtime expand/contract migration pairs (PR #123), batch DML execution with throttling and replication-lag monitoring (PRs #121–#122), and a structured PR review comment generator (#116) all landed before the week was out.

"The revert command now correctly ignores the sqlever:no-transaction directive — a critical correctness fix for production deployments."

The final days pivoted hard toward testing discipline. Seven dedicated test PRs — covering static analysis, Sqitch compatibility, deploy/revert robustness, expand/contract lifecycle, batch DML, AI integration, and security edge cases — were designed, tracked as issues, and merged within hours. Real-world validation issues remain open: five tickets track testing against a live PostgreSQL instance and a compiled binary, ensuring no regressions slip through before the first public release.
