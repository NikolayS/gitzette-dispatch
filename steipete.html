RELEASE

codexbar v0.18.0 adds Kilo, Ollama, OpenRouter, and a forecasting engine that tells you when you'll run out

The menu-bar usage tracker ships its biggest provider expansion yet, plus historical pace risk forecasting for Codex.

Released March 16, codexbar v0.18.0 lands five provider additions in a single cut. Kilo arrives with API/CLI source modes, pass parsing, and zero-balance credit handling. Ollama gets token-account support and Chrome-default auto cookie import. OpenRouter brings credit-based usage tracking with key-quota popups. On the Codex side, historical pace risk forecasting with backfill and zero-usage-day handling gives users an early warning before they blow through their quota. Gemini now splits Pro, Flash, and Flash Lite into separate meters. An experimental option to suppress Claude Keychain prompts rounds out the headline features, and CPU/energy regressions in the JSONL scanner path have been squashed.

COMMUNITY

Alibaba, GPT-5.4 nano, and per-model token counts land in a ten-PR merge blitz

Community contributors delivered a provider, new pricing tiers, and architectural refactors in a single week.

Ten PRs merged this week. #574 Add Alibaba Coding Plan provider extends codexbar into yet another ecosystem. #561 Add GPT-5.4 mini and nano pricing and #511 Preserve exact GPT-5 Codex pricing keys keep the cost engine current. #546 Add per-model token counts to cost history gives users a breakdown they have been asking for. On the plumbing side, #494 Refactor Claude provider planning foundations and #539 Decompose Claude OAuth credentials store collaborators rework internals for what looks like a broader Claude overhaul. #525 fix: show all enabled providers in merged switcher fixes a visibility bug, and #543 Wrap long status blurbs in menu tidies the UI.
